If you are new to production, you must understand **frequency in audio**. Frequency (Hz) is the number of waveform cycles per second and directly shapes how we perceive bass, mids, and highs.

## 1. Human hearing range

Most people hear roughly 20 Hz to 20 kHz, though sensitivity changes across the range.

## 2. Key frequency zones

| Range        | Typical perception            |
| ------------ | ----------------------------- |
| 20-80 Hz     | Sub energy and rumble         |
| 80-250 Hz    | Warmth and body               |
| 250 Hz-2 kHz | Core tone and intelligibility |
| 2-5 kHz      | Presence and clarity          |
| 5-12 kHz     | Brightness and air            |

## 3. Practical vocal use cases

- Muddy vocal: inspect low-mid area first.
- Dull vocal: improve presence zone carefully.
- Harsh vocal: control upper mids and sibilant bands.

## 4. Beginner-safe EQ workflow

1. Cut problem areas first.
2. Make small boosts after cleanup.
3. A/B often at matched loudness.

## 5. FAQ

### Is higher frequency always better?

No. Too much high-end can become harsh and fatiguing.

### Can one EQ preset work for every singer?

No. Voice type, mic choice, and arrangement all change the ideal EQ curve.
