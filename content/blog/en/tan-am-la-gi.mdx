If you want vocals that sound rich and present, you need to understand **harmonics**. Harmonics are frequency components above the fundamental note that define tone color.

## 1. Harmonics explained simply

When you sing one note, the signal contains:

- A fundamental frequency (the main pitch).
- Multiple harmonic frequencies (overtones).

These overtones make one singer sound different from another, even on the same note.

## 2. Why harmonics matter in vocal mixing

- Better note definition in dense arrangements.
- More perceived brightness and texture.
- Easier to place vocals in the mix with less aggressive EQ.

## 3. How to improve harmonic content naturally

### Before recording

- Warm up with humming and lip trills.
- Stay hydrated.
- Choose a comfortable key.

### During recording

- Keep mic distance consistent.
- Open vowels clearly.
- Avoid throat tension.

### During mixing

- Remove mud first (low-mid cleanup).
- Add gentle saturation for harmonic enhancement.

## 4. Useful frequency references

| Band       | Typical perception           |
| ---------- | ---------------------------- |
| 100-250 Hz | Warmth and body              |
| 2-5 kHz    | Presence and intelligibility |
| 8-12 kHz   | Air and openness             |

## 5. FAQ

### Are harmonics the same as pitch?

No. Pitch is the fundamental; harmonics are related overtone components.

### Can plugins replace vocal technique?

Plugins help, but source performance and mic technique are still primary.
