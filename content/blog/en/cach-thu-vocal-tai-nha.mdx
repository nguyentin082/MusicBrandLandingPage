You can absolutely **record vocals at home** with professional results if your workflow is consistent and your signal chain is controlled.

## 1. Minimal gear setup

- Suitable microphone.
- Audio interface with clean preamp.
- Closed-back headphones.
- Pop filter and stable mic stand.

## 2. Gain staging basics

Aim for healthy headroom. A common safe peak target is around -12 to -6 dBFS.

## 3. Performance capture tips

1. Keep mic distance consistent.
2. Record by sections, not only full takes.
3. Tag strong takes during session.

## 4. 7-step home vocal workflow

| Step | Action                     |
| ---- | -------------------------- |
| 1    | Warm up voice and test mic |
| 2    | Set gain and monitor mix   |
| 3    | Record lead by section     |
| 4    | Record doubles/harmonies   |
| 5    | Comp best lines            |
| 6    | Clean timing and breaths   |
| 7    | Export clean stems for mix |

## 5. Common mistakes

- Too far from the mic (thin vocal).
- Too close to the mic (plosives, boominess).
- Inconsistent volume between takes.
- Recording too many random takes without plan.

## 6. FAQ

### Should I use Auto-Tune while tracking?

Track clean first. Tune during post-production for better control.

### Is one full take better than sectional recording?

Sectional recording is usually more efficient and consistent.
